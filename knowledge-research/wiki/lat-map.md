# LAT — Latency engineering (Map of Content)

## Core problem
Voice-to-voice (v2v) latency — the gap from the user's end-of-statement to the agent's start-of-statement — is the figure of merit for a voice engine. Human perception sets hard, non-negotiable thresholds (natural ~300ms, noticeable >500ms, flow-break ~1200ms, hang-up 1–2s), and that ladder, not any engineering target, defines a strict per-turn budget that STT + LLM + TTS + network must share. The job of latency engineering is to fit inside that budget on the **tail** (P95/P99), where conversation actually breaks — by splitting the budget, shrinking and speculating the dominant LLM term, masking what can't be shrunk, and killing the volatile tails of remote providers.

## The narrative
Start with the metric: **v2v is the figure of merit** [[LAT-01-v2v-figure-of-merit]], measured end-to-end (mic-to-speaker), and the only way to manage it is to **instrument each stage** — TTFT for the LLM, TTFB for the TTS, EOU delay for the turn [[LAT-02-per-stage-metrics]]. The budget those stages share is fixed by the **human latency ladder** [[LAT-03-latency-ladder]] (Vapi's 1200ms, Together's 300/500/1-2s, Modal's ~1s). **Splitting that budget** [[LAT-04-turn-budget-split]] shows the LLM is the dominant, variable term (ASR ~300ms / LLM 200–900ms / TTS ~400ms; "LLM majority > TTS > STT"), so latency work is mostly LLM work.

Three families of LLM lever follow. First, **shrink it**: a **TTFT 200–300ms target forces the 8–30B model sweet spot** and engine selection by P95 TTFT [[LAT-05-ttft-target-model-size]]. Second, **route around volatility**: provider latency swings per-deployment-independently, so use **bandit exploit/explore routing** [[LAT-07-bandit-routing]] to ride the fastest endpoint, and **hedged requests with a per-endpoint mean+kσ timeout** [[LAT-06-hedged-requests]] to cancel-and-refire on silent hangs. Third, **take it off the critical path**: **speculative / preemptive generation** [[LAT-09-preemptive-generation]] fires the LLM on a *predicted* end-of-turn so the first token is already in flight, validated-or-scrapped via **predict-and-scrap / greedy inference** [[LAT-10-predict-and-scrap]] when the prediction was wrong (the user never hears the scrapped attempt).

What can't be shrunk gets **masked**: **pre-tool filler speech** (and the thinker–talker pattern) fills airtime while a slow tool/big-model runs in parallel [[LAT-11-filler-speech]]. Underneath all of it, **network latency is a separate budget line**, and **co-location** of orchestrator + STT/TTS/LLM turns a 75ms hop into 5ms (~30% win) [[LAT-08-network-vs-engine-colocation]]. And the whole effort is judged on the **tail (P95/P99)** [[LAT-12-tail-latency]], because a good mean with a 5000ms P99 still makes people hang up.

One more latency line-item deserves its own budget: **guardrail classifier latency** [[LAT-13-guardrail-classifier-latency]]. A pre-TTS content-safety check adds inference time to the critical path per sentence — it must be sized and co-located so its P95 doesn't erase the streaming TTS benefit.

## Open questions / gaps
- **Dynamic per-endpoint hedging threshold (mean+kσ) is unimplemented in any clone** — Vapi only describes it. Needs a from-scratch design fed by [[LAT-02-per-stage-metrics]]; what is the right k, and per-stage or per-endpoint only?
- **Bandit routing** likewise has no OSS reference. Exploration slice sizing vs cost is unspecified.
- **preemptive_tts default-off**: how much extra v2v does speculative TTS actually save vs the wasted-synthesis cost? Needs measurement on our stack.
- **Filler-speech auto-sizing**: ElevenLabs "extends filler to match expected wait" — what predicts the tool's expected latency? (Per-tool historical P50/P95?)
- **Co-location vs elasticity**: Modal's Tunnel bypass loses autoscaling on the inner hop; what is the right pattern for Syrinx to keep both?
- Cross-domain: EOU prediction quality ([[TURN-06-livekit-eou-internals]]) directly bounds how aggressive preemptive generation can be; barge-in cancel sequence ([[BARGE-02-interruption-sequence]]) shares the scrap machinery.
