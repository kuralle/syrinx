**Claim (one line):** Audio preprocessing is the speech-path gatekeeper ‚Äî adaptive thresholding with percentile baselines and neural denoisers (Krisp, RNNoise) clean noisy telephony/microphone audio before it reaches VAD and STT, preventing noise from triggering false turns and degrading recognition.

**Detail.** Vapi's pipeline (vapi-pipeline-2, "Problem #2") describes the most complete adaptive-threshold approach: (1) **baseline tracking** ‚Äî RMS amplitude computed over **3-second rolling windows** in 20 ms chunks; (2) **dynamic thresholds** ‚Äî the **85th percentile** of the audio-level distribution becomes the filtering threshold, updated **every 100 ms via exponential smoothing**; (3) **media detection** ‚Äî when consistent background audio (TV/music) is detected, the system switches to more aggressive filtering with an auto-adjusted baseline offset; (4) **500 ms grace period** prevents clipping the start of words; (5) a **static fallback at ‚àí35 dB** acts as a floor. The core insight: "background speech is typically quieter than the primary speaker," so amplitude plus temporal consistency distinguishes wanted from unwanted voice.

Beyond adaptive thresholding, three OSS clones ship neural noise suppression. **Krisp VIVA SDK** (proprietary, requires `.kef` model + API key) is available in both Pipecat (`krisp_viva_filter.py:35` ‚Äî `KrispVivaFilter(BaseAudioFilter)`, default `noise_suppression_level=100`, `frame_duration=10ms`) and LiveKit Python (`viva_filter.py:58` ‚Äî `KrispVivaFilterFrameProcessor(rtc.FrameProcessor[rtc.AudioFrame])`, plugs into `AudioInputOptions.noise_cancellation`). **RNNoise** (open-source recurrent NN, Mozilla) ships in Pipecat (`rnnoise_filter.py:30` ‚Äî `RNNoiseFilter`, always resamples to 48 kHz since RNNoise requires `sample_rate=48000`, defaults `resampler_quality="QQ"` for lowest latency) and Rapida (`denoiser.go:27` ‚Äî `GetDenoiser()` switches on `microphone.denoising.provider`: `"krisp"` ‚Üí Krisp, default ‚Üí `rn_noise`; Rapida's RNNoise denoiser config is 48 kHz linear16, `rnnoise_denoiser.go:41`,72-74). **Cloudflare** ships no denoiser ‚Äî its `voice-providers` bridge telephony without preprocessing.

**Prior-art divergence.** Vapi's adaptive-threshold approach is not replicated in any OSS clone ‚Äî clones rely on neural denoisers (Krisp/RNNoise) applied as audio filters before VAD, not on percentile-based energy gating. Pipecat applies denoisers as in-pipeline `BaseAudioFilter` processors between transport and VAD; Rapida applies denoising in its packet pipeline before VAD (`denoiser.go` ‚Üí `VoiceDenoiserExecutor`); LiveKit applies it at the WebRTC track level via `FrameProcessor`. The Krisp vs RNNoise tradeoff is quality vs cost: Krisp is vendor-locked but state-of-the-art; RNNoise is free, always 48 kHz, and adds a resampling hop.

**Implication for Syrinx.** Combine: apply a neural denoiser (Krisp or RNNoise) as a configurable filter plug-in, and layer Vapi's adaptive-threshold baseline + media-detection as a statistical guard against background speech specifically. Keep the 500 ms grace period to avoid clipping word onsets. Denoising runs *before* VAD and STT ‚Äî dirty audio in = wrong turns and bad transcripts.
